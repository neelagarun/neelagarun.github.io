I graduated with my Bachelor's in Biology from Boston College in 2022. While there, I conducted 
experimental condensed matter physics research, where I was involved in building nanoscale coaxial 
electrode arrays. We would grow stem cells around these arrays and apply an electric current in 
solution to test the resulting cellular differentiation.

Afterwards, I taught chess at a charter school in New York City.

In 2025, I graduated from Boston University with my MS in Mathematical Finance & Financial 
Technology. While there, I conducted statistical mechanics research in a physics group, where my main 
contributions were in stochastic processes and network science. Interestingly, there were economic 
implications in some of my work, especially in the so-called "Yard-Sale" model.

Since May 2025, I have been involved in a semi-project turned "startup" that my colleague and I began 
with the intent of conducting systematic (aka algorithmic) trading. We focus primarily on statistical 
arbitrage. Most of my work here is focused on alpha research and building machine learning and 
statistical models that help us understand volatility dynamics in the market.

In my free time, I enjoy tennis a lot, and am always interested in playing a match when I can find the time. I've also 
done some work on ML and statistics with the intention of predictive analytics. My favorite player is 
Rafael Nadal.

I also enjoy traveling, and have been to 23 countries so far, which is probably far fewer than most people who claim to
"enjoy traveling." My intention is to visit them all, so we'll see how far I get.
